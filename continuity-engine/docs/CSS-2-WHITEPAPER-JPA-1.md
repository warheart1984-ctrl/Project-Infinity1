# CSS-2 v1.0 — Constitutional Alignment (JPA-1)

## 0. Constitutional Invariant: Judgment Primacy (JPA-1)

CSS-2 v1.0 adopts **JPA-1 — Judgment Primacy** as its deepest continuity invariant.

### JPA-1 — Judgment Primacy Amendment

**JPA-1.1 — Core Principle**  
Continuity is the preserved capacity of a system to exercise **sound, reality-responsive judgment** across lineage.

**JPA-1.2 — Observation as Prerequisite**  
Reality-responsive observation is necessary but not sufficient for sound judgment.  
CSS-2 treats observation as an upstream input to judgment capability.

**JPA-1.3 — Thresholds as Judgment Artifacts**  
Thresholds and Δ-Thresholds are expressions of judgment, not merely rules.  
CSS-2 governs threshold emergence and recalibration as **judgment acts**.

**JPA-1.4 — Stewardship as Judgment Governance**  
Stewardship is governance of judgment capability across generations.  
CSS-2 integrates with JPSS-2 to develop judgment and with CRK-1 to protect its legitimacy.

**JPA-1.5 — Judgment Failure as Ultimate Risk**  
The ultimate continuity failure is loss or corruption of judgment capability, even if observers and rules remain intact.

---

## 1. CSS-2 as a Judgment-Preserving System

CSS-2 v1.0 declares itself a **judgment-preserving system**, meaning:

- It governs how judgments about "what matters" become thresholds
- It governs how judgments are revised through Δ-Thresholds
- It ensures judgments remain reality-responsive through RA-COS-1 evidence
- It ensures judgments remain legitimate through CRK-1 invariants
- It ensures judgment capability is developed through JPSS-2

CSS-2 therefore sits at the center of the lineage-level judgment pipeline:

**Observation → Interpretation → Valuation → Threshold → Δ-Threshold → Stewardship**

CSS-2 governs the middle of this chain, while JPSS-2 develops the upstream capabilities and CRK-1 constrains the downstream legitimacy.

---

## 2. Relationship to OPA-1

CSS-2 remains compliant with **OPA-1 (Observer Primacy)** but treats it as a *contained* principle:

- OPA-1 protects the conditions for reality-responsive observation
- JPA-1 protects the capacity for sound judgment
- CSS-2 requires both, but JPA-1 is the deeper invariant

Thus:

**OPA-1 is necessary for continuity.**  
**JPA-1 defines continuity.**
