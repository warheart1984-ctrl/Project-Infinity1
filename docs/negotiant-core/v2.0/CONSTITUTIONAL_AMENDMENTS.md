# Negotiant Core v2.0 Constitutional Amendments

Version 2.0 explores the evolution from governed runtime to governed multi-agent constitutional substrate. These are proposed amendments, not Version 1.1 guarantees.

## Amendment A: Multi-Core Negotiation

Multiple cores may negotiate lawful transitions under shared constitutional constraints.

Research question: can separate constitutional cores converge on lawful transitions without collapsing disagreement into a single authority?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Multi-core negotiation protocol.
2. Receipts for proposal, counterproposal, acceptance, refusal, and unresolved residue.
3. CTS cases for conflicting lawful transitions.
4. Replay and independent verification reports.

## Amendment B: Distributed Evidence Ledger

Evidence may be distributed across nodes while preserving deterministic verification.

Research question: can consensus-anchored storage coexist with deterministic replay semantics?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Ledger partition model.
2. Content-addressed receipt chain.
3. Fork and merge validation rules.
4. Independent verifier operating from published artifacts.

## Amendment C: Constitutional Objects

Constitutional entities become first-class objects with their own invariants.

Research question: can objects hold constitutional obligations without becoming unbounded agents?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Object schema.
2. Invariant binding rules.
3. Lifecycle receipt model.
4. Challenge protocol.

## Amendment D: Multi-Face Epistemic Arbitration

Faces can disagree, and the system can represent disagreement without forcing premature collapse.

Research question: can disagreement become structured evidence rather than an error state?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Face disagreement schema.
2. Arbitration receipt.
3. Evidence preservation rule.
4. Replayable disagreement examples.

## Amendment E: Temporal Governance

Receipts may schedule future lawful inputs.

Research question: can future obligations be represented without violating present determinism?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Temporal receipt schema.
2. Expiry and activation rules.
3. Replay rules across time boundaries.
4. Independent validation of scheduled transitions.

## Amendment F: Constitutional Proofs

Machine-checkable proofs of invariants become available alongside rule execution.

Research question: where can proofs replace runtime checks, and where must runtime evidence remain primary?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Proof object schema.
2. Proof verifier.
3. Mapping from invariant to proof obligation.
4. Counterexample fixtures.

## Amendment G: Semantic Versioning of Meaning

Interpretive semantics receive explicit versioning, not only algorithms.

Research question: can meaning changes be versioned without freezing interpretation?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Meaning-version manifest.
2. Migration rules.
3. Compatibility tests.
4. Governance receipts for semantic upgrades.

## Amendment H: Negotiated Paradox Resolution

Paradox resolution becomes a governed process instead of a fixed table.

Research question: can negotiated resolution preserve lawful continuity better than fixed resolution tables in multi-agent systems?

Required evidence before promotion:

1. Negotiation protocol.
2. Paradox proposal and settlement receipts.
3. Deadlock and deferral rules.
4. Independent verification of resolution history.

## Version 2.0 Promotion Rule

No amendment becomes normative until it has:

1. A specification.
2. A reference implementation.
3. CTS or equivalent conformance tests.
4. Replay evidence.
5. Independent verification.
6. Governance approval.
