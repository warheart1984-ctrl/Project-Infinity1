# Seven Invariants Canonical Set

Status: **proven**

Authority: Proof-of-Discovery under `pod:jon-halstead`; canonicalized from the Six Invariants / Seven Invariants discovery corpus.

## Final Canonical Set

### Invariant 1 - Identity Preservation

Identity must remain stable, non-recycled, and traceable across all transformations, contexts, and time.

If identity drifts, continuity collapses.

### Invariant 2 - Authority Continuity

Authority must remain consistent, non-escalating, and non-leaking across all actions and transformations.

No silent power gain. No silent scope expansion.

### Invariant 3 - Bidirectional Coherence

Every governed transformation must be reversible without loss, mutation, or drift.

Encode/decode, abstract/concretize, and symbol/meaning transformations must preserve the same identity, authority, and continuity state in both directions.

### Invariant 4 - Symmetric Constraint Surface

Both directions of a dual transformation must operate under the same law surface, constraints, and evaluation criteria.

No asymmetry. No one-way mutation. No irreversible governance gaps.

### Invariant 5 - Evidence Integrity

All continuity-relevant actions must produce verifiable, tamper-evident, reproducible evidence.

No evidence means no event. No event means no continuity.

### Invariant 6 - Law Surface Binding

Every action must declare and satisfy the law surfaces that govern it, including AAIS, CSLEIS, UGR, or mixed law surfaces.

No law means no legitimacy. No legitimacy means no continuity.

### Invariant 7 - The Unifier

All invariants must be simultaneously satisfied for an action to be considered continuous, legitimate, and real.

There is no partial compliance, no "mostly correct", no "close enough", and no skipping invariants.

Continuity equals all invariants satisfied at once.
