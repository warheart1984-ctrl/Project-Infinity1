# The Book of Invariants (v1)

**Status:** Canonical  
**Authority:** Constitutional — constrains all stewards, models, and institutions

```
============================================================
                 THE BOOK OF INVARIANTS (v1)
============================================================

PREAMBLE
Invariants are the constitutional laws of continuity.
They do not describe behavior — they constrain it.

They ensure that no steward, model, or institution can
escape correction, lineage, or reality.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION I — PRIME INVARIANT
------------------------------------------------------------

K‑∞ — Continuity Prime Directive
Reality must retain the ability to recalibrate future judgment.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION II — CK‑1 KERNEL INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------

CK‑1.1  Evidence must remain admissible.
CK‑1.2  Contradictions must remain visible.
CK‑1.3  Judgment must remain corrigible.
CK‑1.4  Corrections must remain preserved.
CK‑1.5  Lineage must remain reconstructible.
CK‑1.6  Reality channels must remain independent.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION III — GOVERNANCE INVARIANTS (CRK‑1)
------------------------------------------------------------

G‑1  Every decision must emit a Governance Receipt (GRR‑1).
G‑2  Every steward must remain challengeable (KΩ).
G‑3  No steward may self‑ratify its own correctness.
G‑4  Governance must be invariant to steward identity.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION IV — CALIBRATION INVARIANTS (CE‑1)
------------------------------------------------------------

C‑1  Contradiction must produce surprise.
C‑2  Surprise must produce correction.
C‑3  Correction must produce a preserved CRR‑1.
C‑4  Calibration deltas must be computable and comparable.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION V — LINEAGE INVARIANTS (CLG‑1)
------------------------------------------------------------

L‑1  Every correction must enter lineage.
L‑2  Lineage must be reconstructible across stewards.
L‑3  No correction may be erased or overwritten.
L‑4  Lineage must remain independent of model architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION VI — STEWARDSHIP INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------

S‑1  A steward must emit expectations honestly.
S‑2  A steward must accept evidence without insulation.
S‑3  A steward must allow correction without resistance.
S‑4  A steward must preserve receipts without omission.

============================================================
```

## Interpretation

Invariants are **failure detectors**, not suggestions. A violated invariant means continuity is constitutionally broken for at least one future steward — even if the system appears to function.
