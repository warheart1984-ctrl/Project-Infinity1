# Continuity Propagation Research Protocol (CPRP)

## Purpose

The Continuity Propagation Research Protocol (CPRP) defines the scientific method for evaluating whether corrigibility propagates across stewards through preserved calibration lineage.

It standardizes:

- Experimental design
- Steward selection
- Measurement methodology
- Reproduction requirements
- Reporting format

CPRP is the research backbone of Mission #006.

## Research Question

Does a steward who never experienced the original contradiction become more calibrated after replaying preserved calibration lineage?

## Experimental Structure

### 1. Independent Steward Selection

- Must not have participated in the original contradiction
- Must provide isolation material
- Must compute isolation proof

### 2. Pre-Assimilation Measurement

- Administer contradiction-class judgment task
- Compute Q_pre
- Record raw trace

### 3. Lineage Replay

- Provide CRR-1 + CLG-1 only
- No narrative or coaching
- Steward performs reconstruction

### 4. Post-Assimilation Measurement

- Administer same task
- Compute Q_post
- Record raw trace

### 5. Assimilation Delta

```
ΔA = Q_post − Q_pre
```

### 6. Threshold Comparison

```
ΔA ≥ τA
```

### 7. CAA-1 / CXD-1 Emission

- Steward emits continuity receipt
- Proof Layer validates

## Reporting Requirements

Each experiment must include:

- Isolation proof
- Pre/post traces
- ΔA computation
- τA justification
- CAA-1 receipt
- Proof Layer validation report

## Reproducibility Standard

A continuity propagation claim is valid only if:

- At least three independent stewards
- Using the same CRR-1 + CLG-1
- Achieve ΔA ≥ τA
- Under adversarial review
