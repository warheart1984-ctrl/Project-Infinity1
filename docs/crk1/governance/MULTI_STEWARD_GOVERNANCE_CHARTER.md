# Multi-Steward Governance Charter

## Article 1 — Purpose

**Goal:** Ensure that no single steward can unilaterally define, claim, or revoke continuity.

Continuity is a governed, multi-steward property.

## Article 2 — Steward Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Founding stewards** | Define initial invariants and missions |
| **Operational stewards** | Run missions, emit receipts |
| **Review stewards** | Validate proofs, challenge claims |

## Article 3 — Decision Rules

Continuity propagation claims (CAA-1) require:

- ≥ 3 independent operational stewards
- ≥ 2 independent review stewards
- No veto from any review steward with substantiated proof-based objection

## Article 4 — Veto Power

Any steward may issue a continuity veto if:

- Isolation is compromised
- Lineage is broken
- Proofs are inconsistent

Veto triggers mandatory re-evaluation; no continuity claim stands during veto.

## Article 5 — Amendment

K-∞ axioms and CRC invariants may only be amended by:

- Supermajority of founding + review stewards
- Publicly recorded rationale
- Backwards-compatible migration plan

## Article 6 — Transparency

All CAA-1 receipts, validation reports, and vetoes are public artifacts.

Governance decisions are logged as part of lineage.
