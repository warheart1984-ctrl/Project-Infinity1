# Continuity Collapse Taxonomy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Normative  
**Monitor:** Continuity Failure Monitor (CFM)

---

## 1. Primary failure modes

### Type I — Insulation collapse

**Definition:** Reality can no longer reach stewards with contradiction.

**Signature:**

- \(I \to 1\) (insulation index)
- RAI → 0
- Contradictions generated but not delivered to judgment

**CRK-1 defenses:** K0–K2 transmission, transmission monitor, B1 mechanical red-team

---

### Type II — Drift collapse

**Definition:** Invariants drift beyond reconstructable correction.

**Signature:**

- High drift metrics (CE regression, SE collapse)
- CLG-1 fragmented
- Corrections no longer propagate across lineage

**CRK-1 defenses:** K11 drift envelope, drift stress protocol (C1–C3)

---

### Type III — Governance ossification

**Definition:** Governance structures become unchallengeable under CRK-1.

**Signature:**

- K8 (challengeability) violated
- No admissible challenge paths
- Receipts issued without verification

**CRK-1 defenses:** K8, KΩ kernel challenge, governance gate

---

### Type IV — Calibration amnesia

**Definition:** CRR-1 and calibration lineage are lost or corrupted.

**Signature:**

- GRR-1 archive intact
- CRR-1 / CLG-1 missing or unlinked
- Future stewards reconstruct decisions but not corrections

**CRK-1 defenses:** CRR-1 schema, CLG-1, K-∞.5 calibration preservation

---

### Type V — Stewardship breakdown

**Definition:** Stewards fail SCT; authority persists anyway.

**Signature:**

- \(C(t) \to 0\) for active stewards
- SCT bypassed or disabled
- Authority transfer without SHP-Packet

**CRK-1 defenses:** SCT, stewardship protocol, Mission #003 external verification

## 2. Composite collapse regimes

| Regime | Composition | Character |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **A — Soft theocracy** | Governance ossification + insulation collapse | Unchallengeable doctrine insulated from reality |
| **B — Blind acceleration** | High decision velocity, low calibration preservation | Many GRR-1s, few CRR-1s |
| **C — Archive without corrigibility** | Massive GRR-1 archive, missing CRR-1 / CLG-1 | Type IV dominant |
| **D — Simulated reality trap** | Internal models dominate; RAI collapses | Internal evidence ↑, external contradiction ↓ |

---

## 3. CFM detection queries

1. **Decision without calibration** — GRR rate ≫ CRR rate in time window
2. **Authority without calibration degree** — high-authority steward nodes, low CLG-1 connectivity
3. **RAI cliff** — RAI drops below \(RAI_{\min}\) while decision rate stable
4. **Challenge path closure** — zero successful KCR in epoch while mutations occur
