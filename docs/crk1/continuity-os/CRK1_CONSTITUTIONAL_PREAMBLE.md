# Continuity OS Preamble

**Status:** Normative  
**Version:** 1.1  
**Scope:** Unified constitutional narrative for Continuity OS

---

## 2.1 Preamble

The purpose of the Continuity Operating System is to **keep the future teachable by reality**.

Continuity is not the preservation of knowledge, doctrine, or structure.  
Continuity is the **persistence of corrigibility** across generations.

A lineage remains continuous for as long as reality retains the power to contradict, surprise, and correct the judgment of its future stewards.

---

## 2.2 Constitutional purpose (K-∞)

At the center of the Continuity OS is a single directive:

**K-∞ — The Continuity Prime Directive**  
The system must preserve reality's ability to recalibrate the judgment of future stewards.

All other invariants, mechanisms, and artifacts exist to protect this capability.

See: [K_INFINITY_CONSTITUTIONAL_COMMENTARY.md](K_INFINITY_CONSTITUTIONAL_COMMENTARY.md)

---

## 2.3 The minimal kernel (CK-1)

The Continuity Kernel (CK-1) defines the minimal conditions under which K-∞ can remain true:

- Evidence must be admissible and trustworthy.
- Contradictions must be visible.
- Judgment must be corrigible.
- Correction paths must exist.
- Calibration must be preserved and traceable.
- Reality channels must remain independently accessible.

CK-1 is the smallest kernel that still allows reality to change future judgment.

See: [CK1_CONTINUITY_KERNEL.md](CK1_CONTINUITY_KERNEL.md), [CONTINUITY_KERNEL_PROOF_SKETCH.md](CONTINUITY_KERNEL_PROOF_SKETCH.md)

---

## 2.4 Governance runtime (CRK-1)

On top of CK-1 sits **CRK-1**, the Constitutional Runtime Kernel.

CRK-1 defines:

- how decisions are made
- how consequences are exposed
- how governance remains challengeable
- how stewards are constrained and empowered

CRK-1 ensures that governance itself remains corrigible to reality, so that K-∞ holds not only for individuals but for the system as a whole.

See: [../CRK1_MINIMAL_RUNTIME_RFC.md](../CRK1_MINIMAL_RUNTIME_RFC.md)

## 2.5 Memory of judgment (GRR-1)

The Continuity OS preserves judgment through:

**GRR-1 — Governance Reconstruction Receipt**

GRR-1 answers:

- What was observed?
- How was it interpreted?
- What values were weighed?
- What action was chosen?
- What outcome occurred?

GRR-1 tells future stewards: **"Why did we think this?"**

It preserves the reasoning that led to past decisions.

---

## 2.6 Memory of correction (CRR-1)

The Continuity OS preserves calibration through:

**CRR-1 — Calibration Reconstruction Receipt**

CRR-1 answers:

- What did we expect?
- What did reality do instead?
- Why was it surprising?
- How did judgment change?
- What calibration was gained?

CRR-1 tells future stewards: **"Why did we stop thinking this?"**

It preserves the exact moments where reality successfully changed judgment.

If K-∞ is the purpose, CRR-1 is its first concrete implementation.

See: [CALIBRATION_LAYER_SPECIFICATION.md](CALIBRATION_LAYER_SPECIFICATION.md)

---

## 2.7 Calibration lineage (CLG-1)

The Continuity OS preserves learning over time through:

**CLG-1 — Calibration Lineage Graph**

CLG-1 links:

- CalibrationEvents (from CRR-1)
- Stewards
- Decisions
- Invariants
- Reality channels

It allows future stewards to trace how invariants were learned, how governance was corrected, and how individual and collective judgment evolved under reality's pressure.

CLG-1 is the structural memory of corrigibility itself.

See: [CLG1_CALIBRATION_LINEAGE_GRAPH.md](CLG1_CALIBRATION_LINEAGE_GRAPH.md)

## 2.8 Unified view

In one stack:

| Layer | Artifact | Role |
|-------|----------|------|
| Purpose | **K-∞** | Why the system exists |
| Kernel | **CK-1** | Minimal conditions for K-∞ |
| Runtime | **CRK-1** | Governance under those conditions |
| Judgment memory | **GRR-1** | Why we thought this |
| Correction memory | **CRR-1** | Why we stopped thinking this |
| Lineage memory | **CLG-1** | How corrigibility evolved |

Together they ensure that:

1. reality can still reach the system
2. reality can still contradict the system
3. reality can still change the system
4. those changes can still be inherited by future stewards

## Continuity chain (operational)

CRK-1 provides the substrate through which:

1. reality produces **evidence**
2. evidence produces **contradiction**
3. contradiction produces **surprise**
4. surprise produces **correction**
5. correction produces **calibration**
6. calibration produces **judgment**
7. judgment produces **stewardship**
8. stewardship preserves **continuity**

---

## Failure modes (constitutional)

When continuity fails, it is because **corrigibility failed**.

When corrigibility fails, it is because **reality lost access to the future**.

CRK-1 exists to prevent that failure.
