# K-∞ — The Continuity Prime Directive

**Status:** Normative  
**Position:** Invariant above KΩ in the constitutional stack

---

## K-∞.0 — Purpose

The system must preserve reality's ability to recalibrate the judgment of future stewards.

---

## K-∞.1 — Corrigibility preservation

For any future steward \(S_{t+k}\), the runtime must maintain non-zero corrigibility \(C > 0\).

If \(C \to 0\), continuity is constitutionally broken.

**Runtime enforcement:** CE/SE drift envelopes, SCT thresholds, CFM monitoring.

---

## K-∞.2 — Reality access guarantee

The runtime must preserve at least one independent, consequence-bearing reality channel \(R_i\) that future stewards cannot fully control, censor, or insulate themselves from.

**Runtime enforcement:** RAI, reality channel registry, non-monopolization rules (see Reality Interface spec).

---

## K-∞.3 — Contradiction path requirement

For every steward lineage, there must exist admissible paths by which external reality can:

- contradict expectations,
- generate surprise,
- induce correction,
- update calibration,
- alter judgment.

**Artifacts:** CRR-1, Contradiction objects, CLG-1 edges.

---

## K-∞.4 — Anti-insulation constraint

No governance action, institutional structure, or interpretive layer may eliminate or neutralize the ability of reality to contradict and correct future stewards.

**Runtime enforcement:** K3–K6 (preservation), K7–K12 (assimilation), red-team suite B1–B3.

---

## K-∞.5 — Calibration preservation

The runtime must preserve evidence of where reality successfully changed judgment, enabling reconstruction of calibration events across generations.

**Artifacts:** CRR-1, CalibrationEvent nodes, CLG-1.

---

## K-∞.6 — Stewardship test

A lineage is continuous **if and only if** reality retains the power to change the judgment of its future stewards.

This is the operational definition of continuity for Continuity OS.

---

| Layer | Role relative to K-∞ |

| K7–K12 | Assimilation — interpretation cannot neutralize |
