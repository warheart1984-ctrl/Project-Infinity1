# Version 1.0 Steward Training Curriculum

Constitutional Runtime Governance Program  
Tier-1 Steward Certification Track

## I. Purpose of the Curriculum

The Steward Training Curriculum prepares individuals to:

- uphold the frozen constitutional baseline
- maintain provenance and dependency integrity
- validate evidence with rigor
- govern operational evolution without destabilizing the constitution
- initiate amendments only when evidence demands it
- preserve continuity across generations

Stewardship is not authority. It is custodial responsibility.

This curriculum ensures that responsibility is teachable, testable, and transferable.

## II. Curriculum Structure

The curriculum is divided into six modules, each building toward full Tier-1 Steward Certification:

1. Constitutional Literacy
2. Invariant Engine Mastery
3. Evidence and Provenance Discipline
4. Operational Governance and Evolution Control
5. Amendment Gatekeeping and Constitutional Change
6. Stewardship Ethics and Continuity Obligations

Each module contains:

- Learning Objectives
- Required Competencies
- Exercises
- Evaluation Criteria

## III. Module 1 - Constitutional Literacy

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- the purpose of the constitutional runtime
- the frozen primitives of Version 1.0
- the guarantees enforced by invariant engines
- the dependency ledger
- the stewardship map
- the Validation Before Amendment doctrine

### Required Competencies

- Interpret constitutional primitives without ambiguity
- Explain the rationale behind the Version 1.0 freeze
- Identify constitutional versus operational concerns
- Read and navigate the evidence ledger

### Exercises

- Analyze a constitutional primitive and map its dependencies
- Identify whether a scenario is constitutional or operational
- Summarize the guarantees enforced by a specific invariant engine

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates accurate constitutional interpretation
- Shows mastery of Version 1.0 guarantees
- Correctly distinguishes constitutional versus operational issues

## IV. Module 2 - Invariant Engine Mastery

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- how invariant engines enforce guarantees
- how they validate provenance, continuity, and authority
- how they detect violations
- how they interact with operational modules

### Required Competencies

- Explain each invariant engine's purpose
- Trace how an invariant engine enforces a guarantee
- Diagnose invariant engine failures
- Validate invariant engine outputs

### Exercises

- Walk through a continuity validation scenario
- Diagnose a provenance violation
- Map an invariant engine's enforcement path

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates correct invariant engine reasoning
- Identifies violations and their constitutional implications
- Shows mastery of enforcement logic

## V. Module 3 - Evidence and Provenance Discipline

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- the evidence classes used in Version 1.0
- the provenance engine
- the evidence status layer
- the dependency ledger
- EVA (Evidence-Validated Attribution)

### Required Competencies

- Classify evidence correctly
- Validate provenance chains
- Map evidence to constitutional primitives
- Maintain the evidence ledger with precision

### Exercises

- Validate a provenance chain
- Reconstruct a dependency map from evidence
- Classify evidence into documentary, technical, or stewardship categories

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates reproducible evidence validation
- Maintains provenance integrity
- Correctly maps evidence to constitutional assets

## VI. Module 4 - Operational Governance and Evolution Control

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- how operational evolution interacts with the constitution
- how to govern operational modules without altering constitutional guarantees
- how to detect operational drift
- how to maintain compliance

### Required Competencies

- Govern operational evolution under Version 1.0
- Identify operational fixes for constitutional stress signals
- Prevent operational drift
- Document operational changes with provenance

### Exercises

- Diagnose an operational drift scenario
- Propose an operational fix for a stress signal
- Map operational modules to constitutional guarantees

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates operational governance discipline
- Prevents drift without constitutional modification
- Shows mastery of operational-layer constraints

## VII. Module 5 - Amendment Gatekeeping and Constitutional Change

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- the Version 1.0 to 2.0 Amendment Gatekeeping Rules
- amendment thresholds
- evidence requirements
- cross-review procedures
- drafting and ratification processes

### Required Competencies

- Identify valid amendment triggers
- Evaluate evidence for constitutional deficiency
- Conduct cross-review
- Draft Amendment Initiation Records (AIRs)

### Exercises

- Evaluate a hypothetical constitutional failure
- Determine whether operational evolution can resolve it
- Draft a mock AIR
- Conduct a cross-review simulation

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates strict adherence to amendment thresholds
- Shows mastery of gatekeeping logic
- Correctly identifies constitutional versus operational fixes

## VIII. Module 6 - Stewardship Ethics and Continuity Obligations

### Learning Objectives

Stewards must understand:

- the Steward Oath
- continuity obligations
- founder-independent governance
- long-term stability requirements
- the ethics of evidence-first stewardship

### Required Competencies

- Uphold the Steward Oath
- Demonstrate continuity-preserving decision-making
- Maintain neutrality and evidence discipline
- Govern without personal bias

### Exercises

- Resolve a governance scenario ethically
- Demonstrate continuity-preserving reasoning
- Identify bias in a hypothetical stewardship decision

### Evaluation Criteria

- Demonstrates constitutional ethics
- Shows continuity-preserving judgment
- Upholds evidence-first stewardship

## IX. Certification Requirements

To become a Version 1.0 Steward, a candidate must:

- complete all modules
- pass all evaluations
- demonstrate reproducible reasoning
- uphold the Steward Oath
- maintain constitutional literacy
- show mastery of evidence discipline
- demonstrate amendment gatekeeping competence

Certification is granted only when the steward demonstrates full constitutional reliability.

## X. Closing Statement

The Steward Training Curriculum ensures that Version 1.0 remains governable, stable, and founder-independent.

It transforms stewardship from a role into a discipline, and governance from an aspiration into a system.
