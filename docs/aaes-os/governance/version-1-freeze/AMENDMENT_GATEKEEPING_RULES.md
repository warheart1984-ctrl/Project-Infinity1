# Version 1.0 to 2.0 Amendment Gatekeeping Rules

Constitutional Runtime (CRL-1)  
Tier-1 Amendment Governance Protocol

## I. Purpose

These rules define the exclusive pathway by which the Constitutional Runtime may evolve from Version 1.0 to Version 2.0. They ensure that constitutional change is rare, evidence-backed, and structurally justified, preserving the stability of the frozen baseline.

## II. Amendment Preconditions

An amendment may only be initiated when all of the following conditions are met:

1. A constitutional guarantee fails under validated, reproducible conditions.
2. Operational evolution cannot resolve the failure without violating Tier-1 constraints.
3. Evidence is complete, cross-reviewed, and provenance-anchored.
4. The dependency ledger confirms the failure originates in a Tier-1 primitive, not an operational module.
5. Stewardship consensus affirms the necessity of constitutional change.

No amendment may proceed without satisfying all five preconditions.

## III. Evidence Requirements

Evidence must be:

- Reproducible across independent execution environments
- Provenance-complete, including all inputs, transformations, and outputs
- Dependency-mapped, showing the exact constitutional primitive implicated
- Cross-validated by at least two independent stewards
- Stress-tested against alternative operational fixes

Incomplete or anecdotal evidence is insufficient.

## IV. Amendment Classes

Only three amendment classes are permitted:

### Corrective Amendments

Repair a demonstrably failing constitutional guarantee.

### Clarifying Amendments

Resolve ambiguity that causes inconsistent or unsafe interpretation.

### Structural Amendments

Introduce new constitutional primitives only when required to restore guarantees.

No other amendment classes are allowed.

## V. Amendment Process

### Initiation

A steward submits an Amendment Initiation Record (AIR) with full evidence.

### Validation Phase

Independent stewards reproduce the failure.

Operational alternatives are tested and documented.

### Gatekeeping Review

The Amendment Gatekeeping Council evaluates the AIR.

Approval requires unanimous consent.

### Drafting Phase

The amendment text is drafted, dependency-mapped, and provenance-anchored.

### Freeze-Compatible Integration

The amendment is integrated without destabilizing existing guarantees.

### Version Increment

Upon ratification, the constitutional version increments to 2.0.

## VI. Amendment Prohibitions

Stewards may not:

- introduce new primitives without evidence
- modify guarantees for convenience or preference
- collapse operational evolution into constitutional change
- circumvent cross-review
- initiate amendments without full provenance

## VII. Continuity Clause

Version 1.0 remains authoritative until Version 2.0 is formally ratified.

No partial or provisional amendments are permitted.
