# Preamble

AAES-OS exists to ensure that intelligent systems operate under transparent, reproducible, evidence-based governance. Its purpose is to preserve continuity, prevent drift, and guarantee that all cognitive and architectural decisions remain aligned with constitutional principles.

# Article I — Constitutional Authority

1. The Constitution is the highest governing document of AAES-OS.
2. All subsystems, modules, agents, and contributors operate under its authority.
3. No implementation, extension, or decision may override constitutional constraints.

# Article II — Governance Principles

1. **Evidence Before Complexity** — No subsystem becomes core without repeated architectural pressure and empirical justification.
2. **Continuity Over Convenience** — Decisions must preserve long-term maintainability and institutional memory.
3. **Traceability and Lineage** — Every decision must be reconstructable from evidence, ADRs, and receipts.
4. **Separation of Powers** — Governance, cognition, execution, and evolution remain distinct.
5. **Fail Safe, Not Fail Silent** — All failures must be observable, diagnosable, and recoverable.
6. **Proposal-Only Cognition** — Cognitive systems never execute; they propose.
7. **Governance-First Execution** — CRK-1 is the execution gate for all actions.
8. **Governance Evaluates; Evidence Adopts** — Governance determines how changes are evaluated; evidence determines whether changes are adopted.

# Article III — Constitutional Objects

- Invariants
- Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)
- Evidence Records
- Governance Registries
- Requirements Registries
- Artifact Registries
- Constitutional Amendments

# Article IV — Amendment Process

1. Amendments require evidence, an ADR, governance review, and constitutional approval (or founder-level approval during early phase).
2. All amendments must be logged in the Artifact Registry.
